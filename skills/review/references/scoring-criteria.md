# Review — Detailed Scoring Criteria

## Investor Lens: Full Rubric

What a serious investor notices in the first 30 seconds:

```
□ Can this be explained clearly in one sentence?
□ Is the market meaningfully large?
□ Why this team?
□ Why now?
□ Is the business model understandable?
□ Could this become a large business, or is it inherently capped?
□ What are the first 3 risks that would go into an investment memo?
```

### Scoring breakdown

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| Strong | Market >$100M, defensible moat, team has track record, business model is clear, path to $10M+ ARR exists |
| Needs Work | Market is real but smaller, model needs clarity, execution risk is moderate, one of these can be fixed with iteration |
| Pass | Market too small or too crowded, business model is flawed at core, team gaps are unfixable, idea depends on a single unproven assumption |

---

## User Lens: Full Rubric

What the actual user feels on first contact:

```
□ Do I understand what this does in 5 seconds?
□ Does it solve a real problem for me?
□ Is it better than what I already use?
□ What would make switching worth it?
□ Would I pay for it?
□ Would I recommend it?
□ What would make me stop using it after a week?
```

### Scoring breakdown

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| Would use daily | Solves immediate pain, beats alternatives by 2+ dimensions, frictionless onboarding, clear ROI in <1 week |
| Would try once | Interesting concept, solves a real problem, but switching cost is high or benefit is modest |
| Would ignore | Unclear value prop, easier to stick with current tool, feels like a feature not a product, no urgent reason to switch |

---

## Competitor Lens: Full Rubric

What the strongest competitor would think:

```
□ Can this be copied quickly?
□ Do they already have distribution or data to do it better?
□ Is there a real moat here?
□ Where is the weakest point?
□ What would a strong competitor build to neutralize it?
□ Is this easier to copy, ignore, or acquire?
```

### Scoring breakdown

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| Threatening | Real moat (data, network effects, brand), distribution advantage, hard to replicate in <12 months, competitor must respond immediately |
| Annoying | Copyable in 6-9 months, no durable moat, but grabs share first-mover premium before response |
| Ignorable | Trivial to copy or obsolete by adjacent tech, low strategic threat, not worth a competitor's development budget |

## Financial Verification Checklist

If numbers are present, check them all:

```
□ Revenue assumptions realistic?
  └─ Based on what? Market size? Pricing precedent? Pilot data?

□ CAC based on what?
  └─ Channel costs? Actual pilot? Benchmarked industry rates?

□ LTV based on what churn or retention assumption?
  └─ How did they derive retention? From comparable products? Pilot?

□ Margin includes real costs?
  └─ COGS, ops, support, payment processing, payment, hosting all accounted for?

□ Breakeven timeline grounded or hand-wavy?
  └─ What ramps by when? Traction curve plausible or hope-based?

□ Every number tagged:
  ├─ [confirmed] — from audit, pilot data, customer contracts
  ├─ [estimated] — based on industry benchmarks or prior experience
  └─ [unverified] — assumption pending validation
```

**Rule**: Do not let clean formatting disguise weak economics.

## Fact-Based Claims Mapping

Good review should distinguish clearly:

### Examples of sound support

```
✓ "Based on X's public pricing, this seems plausible"
✓ "This assumption depends on Y, which is unproven"
✓ "This number is estimated, not validated"
✓ "Assuming 3% monthly churn (industry median), LTV would be..."
✓ "Pilot showed 12% conversion; scaling to 1M users would cost..."
```

### Examples of unsupported language (red flag)

```
✗ "Huge market"
✗ "Users will love this"
✗ "Strong moat" (with no mechanism)
✗ "Uniquely positioned" (with no evidence)
✗ "First-mover advantage" (as sole defensibility)
✗ "Network effects" (if not demonstrated in pilot)
```

**Rule**: Every important claim should either have support or be labeled clearly as an assumption.

# Seed Stage — Clarify the Basic Shape

This is where you answer: What is the product, who is it for, and what problem does it solve?

## Core Questions

```
- What is the product in one sentence?
- Who is it for?
- What painful or expensive problem does it solve?
- Why is the current alternative not good enough?
- What is the smallest useful version of it?
```

## What You're Looking For

**One-sentence product definition**
Clear, specific, avoids marketing language. Testable.

**Target user**
Be narrow. "Everyone" is not an answer. Name a specific role, company size, or use case.

**Core problem**
Quantify if possible: "Takes 6 hours per week", "Costs $50K annually", "95% fail rate."
Not "frustrating" or "annoying." Describe the cost of living with the problem.

**Why alternatives fail**
Why can't they use spreadsheets, manual processes, or existing tools?
What specifically is missing?

**Smallest useful version**
This is not "the full vision." It's the version that solves the core problem with minimal features.
It should be shippable in one sprint.

## Red Flags

- Idea is still fuzzy after this stage → return to narrowing, do not proceed to Market Framing
- Target is too broad ("everyone who works") → pick one department, one company size, one workflow
- Problem is vague ("people want better X") → your next action is customer conversations, not product work
